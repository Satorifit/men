The mechanism

It’s not inflammation.
It’s structural.

01

The cable

Every tendon in your body is a tightly braided cable of thousands of collagen fibers locked together in tension. Past 35, it frays faster than your body rebuilds it. The cable thins. That’s the structural damage you feel as “tendinitis.”

02

The cell that rebuilds it

Inside every tendon there’s one cell type responsible for laying down new collagen and cross-linking it into the cable. The fibroblast. When fibroblasts are active, tendons remodel. When they’re dormant, tendons degrade.
